Album Spotlight

The Velvet Rope Janet Jackson

This week we're spotlighting Janet Jackson's 1997 album, The Velvet Rope, which is set to celebrate its 25th anniversary on October 7th. Conceived from Janet's own experience with depression, the album's 22 tracks explore themes of loneliness, loss, sexuality, and self-worth, and it is largely cited as her most personable and introspective work to date. Aside from hits like Together Again and I Get Lonely, album highlights include Got Til' It's Gone, a trip-hop record featuring Q-tip and an infectious sample of Joni Mitchell's Big Yellow Taxi, and Go Deep, a funky dance record that'll "keep you up all night until the early light."

Throwbacks

Faith George Michael

Throwback to October 30th, 1987 when George Michael, released his first studio album, Faith, since departing from the pop-duo Wham! and becoming a solo act. The debut album would go on to sell over 25 million copies internationally, solidifying it as one of the best selling albums of all time.
Genres : Pop / Pop-rock / Soul
